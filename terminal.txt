What does the man command do? Type in man rm. How do you scroll and get out?

- man stands for manual. 
- you can scroll normally or with W,D and you get out of it with q.

Look at the man page for ls. What does the -l flag do? What does the -a flag do?

- a flag = include all directory entries that begin with "." 
- l flag = includes more details on files in directory, each line is a file.

How do you jump between words in the terminal?

- you can hold option and click to move your cursor, or option left/right

How do you get to the end of a line in terminal?

- control e

How do you move your cursor to the beginning in terminal?

- control a

How do you delete a word (without pressing backspace multiple times) in terminal?

- control w

What is the difference between a terminal and shell?

- shell handles the commands 
- terminal is the program that contains/runs shell

What is an absolute path?

- The direct path to where you are, starts at root.

What is an relative path?

- path starts at current location

What is a flag? Give three examples of flags you have used.

- flags add additional functionality to a command.
- rm -f = attempt to remove file without asking permission
- rm -r(put these two together) = attempt to remove file hierarchy rooted in each file argument.
- ls al = performs a and l together

What do the r and f flags do with the rm command?

- Using both together will remove the directory and its contents
